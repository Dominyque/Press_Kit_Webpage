Project Description ⮋⮉

Charlatman Gyroller is a collectathon physics based casual platformer for mobile.

Mobile is a platform whose full range of tools are often overlooked, but when implemented can bring astonishing results as an example Niantic Inc’s “Ingress”, which was a basis for world encapsulating phenomena - “Pokemon Go”. In my case the gyroscope as input for 3d movement solves the lack of feedback regular touch screen interfaces provide, when compared to buttons, as constant movement of the wrist emphasizes the holding of your phone, action on its own decreasing and emphasizing the distance between the player and game – that is the essence of an art medium. The core game itself a casual collectathon – rolling a ball in a track finding and taking mcGuffins until finish is reached where your points are calculated and by watching an ad the next level is opened. Because the game is physics based and the mechanics of tilting the phone are new to the player the terrain itself acts like challenge the difficulty can be measured in ruggedness and angles, future development may include wind resistance, water and buoyancy, gravity flip and bounciness added.

Whilst casually progressing through the game I expect the player wanting to tweak the controls or camera, here I bring my design twist and, what I intend to be, the hook of the game. The “Charlatmanual” is a mechanic presented as a book whose pages are interactive but not intuitive menus in order to alter the camera, pellet’s physical properties and other game features, you exchange collected points to material properties in the book that change the material properties of the pellet, some pages are locked depending on game progression.

The story is similarly not announced overtly but is found in the menu. As some people just never bother to read stories, and others read any bits designers throw at them, here I decided to candidly explain the background lore. I want to create a dialogue like prose in order to make what is essentially a lore dump more lively. So a charlatman is s class similar to an alchemist combined with a musketeer that long ago killed all the dragons, the player is a charlatman and the game is representation of progression in charlatman craft and the last recollections of the dragons.

a Fiscal Paradox ⮋⮉

The mobile game market remains as the most profitable entertainment venture in the market. While the gacha and other pseudo gambling style games are the most profitable at the moment, breaking into a saturated market would require an expansive development team and vast knowledge in order to be competitive with established titles and players, so for my lack of experience – my break through example is an older type of game and monetization model of a free to play ad-locked game.

I completed a working demo sized game portion and released it to the wind of google play. Then I invested some over 100$ to ads and waited for results to get a better understanding about the monetization model. Results were underwhelming and I still cant find a suitable explanation for the following problem.

Over 1000 people have downloaded the game and presumably played it,
the first level is an introduction to the physical mechanics and is not a challenge, after which an ad is requested from the ads provider via an API call,
the requests for ads numbered in the 10’s to the contrast of 1000 players.
Possible explanations: (a) no ads are available to the regions of my ad campaign targeted player base; (b) everyone is using ad blockers of VPN’s that interfere with the API calls; (c) the low budget targeting results in an acquisition of fictitious users (mobile bot farms). I have rejected the following explanations: first level being too difficult/unfinishable for most players or them getting boded/dropping the game – it takes too fast of a time to complete the level to even make an assessment; people just don’t want to see ads and quit after seeing one – I have counted the ad requests, not clickes.

Hopefully your company can provide me with further knowledge to successfully draw players in – I don’t know which necessary part of video game development/marketing I lack the most.
Releasing a console version (nintendo switcch and playstation 5 have gyroscopes, to my knowledge).
I am open to your input, actually, I am hungry for it.

My current expectation is to get financial provisions for a year of development and to spend double of that amount for marketing.
30k(sudgested investment fund) - 10k(living expense for a year) = 20k(marketing budget)
